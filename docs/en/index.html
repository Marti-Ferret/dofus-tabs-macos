Why this exists

Windows has half a dozen of these tools. Mac has none that work well.

We researched the whole ecosystem before writing a line of code: seven active projects, almost all C#/WinUI, none for Mac except a 2024 attempt built with Electron and AppleScript, abandoned after seven commits.

7 / 7

Serious tools in the Dofus ecosystem are Windows-only.

2024

Last activity on the only native macOS attempt that existed.

AXUIElement

No AppleScript: focus and tiling through the Accessibility API, instant.

Features

Just enough to run 4, 6, or 8 accounts without losing a second.

Automatic detection

Finds your Dofus windows on its own, no setup needed — via the Accessibility API, not a fragile ps aux script.

Real thumbnails

A small screenshot of each window, not a generic icon — you know who's who at a glance.

Shortcuts your way

Click a shortcut and press whatever combination you want. The cycle, the tiling, and every character are reassignable.

Arrange into a grid

One shortcut spreads every active window across the screen — no dragging by hand.

Exclude your mules

Take an account out of the shortcut rotation without closing it — it stays in the menu, just out of the cycle.

Starts on its own

Turn it on once and it's waiting in the menu bar every time you start your Mac.

In practice

From install to playing, three steps.

Open it once

Grant Accessibility and Screen Recording permission — the two it needs to see and move your windows.

Open your accounts

Each character shows up on its own in the menu, with its thumbnail and assigned shortcut.

Play with the keyboard

⌘1–⌘9 to jump straight there, ⌘0 to arrange everything on screen.

Before installing

Requirements and permissions

  • System macOS 13 (Ventura) or later, Apple Silicon or Intel chip.
  • Accessibility required — without it, it can't detect or focus Dofus windows.
  • Screen Recording optional — it still works without it, just without character thumbnails.
  • Signature ad-hoc, not Developer ID — the first launch needs confirming with right-click → Open.
